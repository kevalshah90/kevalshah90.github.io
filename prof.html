My Story

My professional career began when I landed my first job in marketing working as a Developer building mobile products. Through my work, I worked closely with Consumer data. It got me curious about the field and I started researching about data science. I made the decision to pursue graduate studies in Analytics at University of Chicago and was one of the first to enroll into the program.

During graduate school, I had the opportunity to travel to Japan on a NSF Research Fellowship to work at . My research work was focused on mining social media text, temporal and location data to build a visual analytics tool. The goal was to understand geo-temporal patterns in topics of interests.

For the last year of graduate school, I was holding down a corporate job in Advertising working full time and taking classes in the evening. On paper, my life was fairly successful. However, I didn't particularly feel accomplished and didn't quite understand what was missing(Perhaps, my twitter has some hints). Upon graduating, I decided to take some time off and travel across Europe to France, Austria and Switzerland. I spent the next year working in different industries, pursuing my interest in Econometrics and taking time to travel more. I worked in Finance, Market Research and Public Policy.

My interests are Economics and Econometric theory, Machine Learning, and Experimental Methods. These days I'm reading Philosophy, Psychology and Economics Books.

I live in San Francisco, CA.
